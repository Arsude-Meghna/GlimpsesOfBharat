KERALA- 'God's Own Country'

Throughout its history, Kerala has been a center of trade and commerce in South Asia, where India is located. Many of its cities are on the coast. Meanwhile, the Western Ghats mountain chain rises on the eastern edge of Kerala, serving as a border that isolates Kerala somewhat from the rest of India. There is some debate over the exact origin of the name Kerala, which in the ancient period was known as Keralam. One theory argues it originates from the Chera dynasty, an ancient lineage of kings that ruled in modern-day Kerala. Alternatively, the name Kerala may have originated in the word for coconut, keram, in the local Malayalam language (Kerala's tropical climate allows for the growth of coconuts).

*TRADITIONAL ATTIRE-

1) A mundu usually has a line of comparatively thicker cloth woven into it near the border called the kara. The kara can be coloured and comes in various sizes. There is also double coloured and ornamental kara (a strip of colour at the end of the mundu). For more ceremonial occasions (like weddings), a mundu has a golden embroidery known as kasavu.

Mundu

2) A variant called a mundum neriyatum is used more often by women. The mundum neriyatum is a set of two mundus, both having matching kara. The set contains a lower garment similar to those worn by men. The upper mundu, worn with a blouse, is wrapped once around the waist and upper body and left hanging from the left shoulder, resembling a sari.

Mundum Neriyathum
